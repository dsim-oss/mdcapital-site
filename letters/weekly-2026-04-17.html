01

Macro Environment

The week was shaped less by crypto-native flows than by the macro tape. Monday's CPI print came in cooler than expected and reset the rate-cut calendar a notch tighter, which showed up cleanly in risk assets midweek. The U.S.–Iran ceasefire reportedly took effect, removing some of the geopolitical risk premium that had dragged BTC down to the $70k handle earlier in the month. By Thursday and Friday the headline tape had quieted enough for spot to grind higher even as sentiment lagged.

The persistence of extreme fear in the face of a 7% BTC rally is, in our reading, the most important macro fact of the week. Either the market is mispricing the move — in which case the setup remains accumulation-favorable — or the rally fades on the next negative print. Both paths leave us comfortable with the trades we put on.

Bitcoin This Week

Spot BTC traded a range of $70,889 to $75,412, closing today near the weekly high. The Fear & Greed index oscillated 14 → 23 across the week and never exited the extreme-fear band; sentiment-implied positioning remains thin. Spot BTC ETFs printed approximately $871M of inflows on April 13 alone, reversing a tepid trailing-30-day pace and putting institutional bid back under the market.

Our BTC accumulation signal (Fear & Greed ≤ 20) fired on Tuesday at a print of 14 while spot was rallying — the kind of capitulation-then-bounce setup that historically pays on a 90- to 180-day window. We added to Swan during this window. We are watching for a sub-$70k retest or a fresh sub-20 sentiment print to deploy the next tranche.

03

Featured: Strategy’s STRC Engine

The most consequential institutional development of the week was almost mechanical: between April 6 and April 12, Strategy added 13,927 BTC at an average price of approximately $71,902, a roughly $1B purchase funded almost entirely through their STRC preferred-stock at-the-market facility. The ATM pulled in $1.76B of new liquidity this week and STRC closed the week within pennies of par. Total Strategy holdings now sit at 780,897 BTC, with a cumulative cost basis near $58B.

This is the cleanest illustration we’ve had of the STRC flywheel in action. Strategy issues preferred stock to yield-seeking institutional buyers, takes the proceeds, buys spot BTC, and the resulting balance-sheet expansion is the input to MSTR common’s intrinsic NAV. When MSTR common trades at a discount to that NAV — as it has all week, at roughly −19% — the equity is, in effect, BTC at a markdown. The STRC dividend was confirmed at 11.5% annualized for April, payable April 30, which remains the structural reason we hold STRC at all: dollar-yielding instrument that funds the BTC accumulation we believe in.
