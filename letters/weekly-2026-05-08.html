The Eleven — Performance Metric Dashboard

Eleven metrics, each measuring something distinct, none double-counting. Together they decompose the fund across the dimensions that matter: BTC accumulation, real cash income, breakeven risk, execution discipline, and how much of the natural yield buffer is being used by any opportunity-cost drag.

−1.3%
0.2240 BTC actual vs 0.2270 BTC alt
Compares the BTC-equivalent of the portfolio today against what we’d hold if every dollar we deployed into MSTR or STRC had instead bought spot BTC on that exact fill date. Time-weighted — the comparison uses the BTC price available on each deployment day, not today’s price applied retroactively. This is the keystone metric: did the blend beat just buying BTC? Negative means we’re paying a small price for the income engine; positive would mean the blend earned its keep on growth too.

Macro Environment

The week was about events that didn’t move markets. Strategy reported Q1 on Tuesday morning — the print came in roughly as expected, the diluted share count update tightened our MSTR-NAV math without changing the story, and the stock did very little on the day. Sentiment leading price into the next reaction.

The single dated event still in front of us this week is April CPI on May 13. A cooler print supports the rally; a hotter print could test the $76,000–$78,000 zone and bring the BTC accumulation signal back into play. We are not pre-positioning. We are watching.

Why We Bought

Two reasons, weighted in roughly that order:

  • Cash sleeve was structurally overweight. At last Friday’s mark, cash was 10.8% of NAV vs. a notional target of roughly 5%. The $1,550 deployment moved the cash weight to ~2% — overshooting in the other direction, but acceptable given the BTC sleeve was simultaneously 49% (under-target). Net rebalance brought BTC to 57% (over-target now) and cash to 2% (under-target), which we’ll trim back over time as STRC distributions accumulate.
  • Pre-CPI optionality. April CPI prints Tuesday May 13. The desk preferred to enter BTC at $81,876 / $81,962 rather than wait for a potential post-print rally to $85k or sit through a potential pullback to $76k with cash earning 0%. Doctrine call: when no signal is firing in either direction, deployment of structurally idle cash into the base layer is acceptable.

This was a discretionary call, not a signal-triggered one. We are flagging it explicitly for the record.

When in doubt, do less. Document before execute. The benchmark is BTC, not dollars. Size to the signal, not the headline. No leverage on spot BTC, ever.

Risks We Are Watching

  • Cash sleeve is now thin. $413 total cash (across IBKR and bank) is below our usual operating reserve. A signal that fires in the next two weeks would have to be acted on against a smaller dry powder pile. Trade-off accepted — we converted cash drag into BTC exposure — but worth flagging that we’ve traded one constraint for another.
  • OC scoreboard cushion is comfortable but compresses on rallies. The Tolerance Utilization metric (#11) reads 16% — we’re using 16% of the natural yield buffer. The threshold to watch is 50% utilization; above that, the book’s deployment policy needs an active adjustment.
  • STRC June rate-set. Calibrated against May VWAP, which is shaping up sub-par again on the same persistent below-$100 print pattern. A rate step-down at the next reset would lower the income leg’s yield contribution and raise the OC tolerance utilization. Tracking the May VWAP daily.
  • CPI binary risk. Tuesday’s print is the single asymmetric event of the next two weeks. With cash now thin, both outcomes are framework-handleable but the cooler-print scenario (rally extends, OC drag widens, cash is stuck on the sidelines) is the less comfortable one. The hotter-print scenario (pullback, signal fires) becomes harder to act on without freeing capital from existing positions.
