MD Capital Investment Thesis v2.0
Last updated: March 2026 · Damir Simunac, Managing Member

1. Core Conviction

Bitcoin is a monetary network undergoing a phase transition from speculative asset to global reserve collateral. The macro tailwinds — sovereign adoption, corporate treasury strategies, spot ETF inflows, and persistent monetary debasement — create asymmetric upside on a 3-5 year horizon. MD Capital positions across the full BTC capital structure to capture this transition.

2. The Strategy (MSTR/STRC) Thesis

Key Insight: Strategy Inc. has invented a new financial primitive — using equity and preferred stock issuance to accumulate Bitcoin, creating a self-reinforcing flywheel where capital markets access becomes a BTC acquisition engine.

As of March 2026, Strategy holds ~738,750 BTC (~$50B+). Their capital structure offers three distinct instruments:

  • MSTR (Common): Levered BTC upside with NAV premium/discount dynamics. Trades as a high-beta BTC proxy. Currently at a significant discount to NAV — a potential accumulation signal.
  • STRC (Perpetual Preferred): $100 par, 11.5% variable dividend, paid monthly. Designed to trade near par with income. Provides high yield income while maintaining BTC-correlated exposure through the issuer.
  • Convertible bonds: Not held by MD Capital currently, but worth monitoring for future opportunistic entry.

3. Our Edge

  • Full Capital Structure Play: Most BTC investors hold spot only. We diversify across spot BTC (direct exposure), STRC (income + stability), and MSTR (convexity). Each instrument serves a different portfolio function.
  • Income Generation: 35.28 shares of STRC at 11.5% = ~$406/year in dividends. This income stream partially offsets BTC volatility and accelerates Protected Capital repayment.
  • NAV Premium Arbitrage: When MSTR trades at a deep discount to NAV (as now), it offers BTC exposure at a discount. When at premium, we rotate to spot. This systematic approach adds alpha vs buy-and-hold.
  • Systematic Signal Framework: Rather than emotional trading, our macro + technical signal engine defines three regimes (Offensive / Neutral / Defensive) with pre-defined allocation adjustments.

4. Market Regime Framework

OFFENSIVE (Score ≥ 6/10 bullish signals): Lean into risk. Target 55-60% BTC, 25-30% equities, 10-20% opportunistic. Consider adding MSTR on dips.

NEUTRAL (Score 4-5/10): Hold steady at IPS targets. 50% BTC, 25% equities, 25% opportunistic. Harvest STRC income, no aggressive moves.

DEFENSIVE (Score ≤ 3/10): Reduce risk. Tighten to 40-45% BTC, 20% equities, rest in stables/hedges. Sell covered calls on MSTR if IV is elevated.

5. Risk Factors

  • BTC Drawdown Risk: A 50%+ BTC correction would significantly impair NAV. Stress test: at $35k BTC, portfolio NAV drops to ~$5.4k. STRC provides partial cushion via continued dividends.
  • Strategy Concentration Risk: MSTR and STRC are both Strategy Inc. instruments. If Strategy faces a credit event, both positions are impaired simultaneously. Mitigation: STRC has seniority over common equity.
  • Regulatory Risk: Adverse crypto regulation could impact BTC price and Strategy's business model. Diversification across spot and equity mitigates somewhat.
  • Liquidity Risk: With ~$14.7k total NAV (including $4k cash), position sizes are small but proportionally concentrated. Any single position exit is manageable at current sizes.
  • Protected Capital Obligation: Mike's $20k (two deposits) + 5% annual return must be prioritized. Current NAV is below the Protected Capital threshold — this needs monitoring and a clear repayment plan.

6. Growth Path

MD Capital's next milestones:

  • Scale AUM through additional member contributions or LP admission

Trade Playbook — Pre-Defined Actions

These are pre-approved actions you can execute when triggers fire. No emotion, just execution.

PLAY 1: BTC Accumulation (Offensive)
Trigger: Fear & Greed ≤ 20 AND BTC below 200D SMA AND MSTR at NAV discount
Action: Deploy available cash to spot BTC via Swan. If no cash, consider selling STRC shares at par to buy BTC (only if conviction is high). Log in Allocation Tracker.
PLAY 2: MSTR Discount Accumulation
Trigger: MSTR NAV premium < -15% (trading at significant discount to BTC holdings)
Action: Accumulate MSTR shares on IBKR. You're effectively buying BTC at a discount. Size: up to 5% of NAV per entry.
PLAY 3: STRC Income Harvest
Trigger: STRC trading at or below $100 par with dividend rate ≥ 10%
Action: Add STRC shares on IBKR for income. The yield acts as a consistent return floor. Monthly dividends compound or fund future BTC purchases.
PLAY 4: Covered Call Income (Neutral/Defensive)
Trigger: MSTR IV Percentile ≥ 70 AND market regime is Neutral or Defensive
Action: Sell covered calls on MSTR at 20-30% OTM, 30-45 DTE. Premium income reduces cost basis. Only if holding 100+ shares (future consideration at current position size).
PLAY 5: Defensive Rotation
Trigger: Bear signal composite (≤ 3/10 bull signals) OR BTC breaks below 200D SMA by 20%+
Action: Reduce MSTR position by 50%. Rotate proceeds to STRC (income preservation) or spot BTC (if conviction remains). Increase cash/stablecoin allocation. Log reasoning.
PLAY 6: Rebalance (Quarterly)
Trigger: End of Q1, Q2, Q3, Q4 — or when any allocation drifts > ±10% from IPS targets
Action: Review allocation vs IPS (50/25/25). Trim overweight positions, add to underweight. Write quarterly memo in Allocation Tracker. Compare performance vs. pure BTC benchmark.
