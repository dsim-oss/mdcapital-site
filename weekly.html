Mike, happy Friday. It has been a week defined by macro headwinds rather than crypto-specific weakness — and that distinction matters for how we think about what comes next. Bitcoin is down roughly 6.6% on the week, but the broader context paints this as a macro-driven repricing, not a structural deterioration. The Fear & Greed Index has now sat in Extreme Fear territory for 46 consecutive days — the longest such streak since the FTX collapse in late 2022 — and history strongly favors patient holders in environments like this one.

01

Macro Environment

🛢️

Iran Conflict & Oil Shock

US-Israel strikes on Iran began February 28. Brent crude is up more than 40% since, trading near $104/barrel this week. The IEA calls this the largest supply disruption in oil market history. Markets whipsawed on Monday when Trump announced a temporary halt to strikes on Iranian energy infrastructure, then gave most of it back by Thursday. Elevated energy costs feed directly into inflation and complicate the Fed's path forward.

🏦

Fed: Rates on Hold, Inflation Forecast Raised

The FOMC held rates at its March 18 meeting — second consecutive hold of 2026. Updated projections raised the 2026 inflation forecast to 2.7% (up from 2.5%) and still pencil in just one 25bp cut for the full year. The longer-run neutral rate was nudged to 3.1%. Higher-for-longer is now the baseline, and the oil shock makes early cuts even less likely. Historically, this type of environment benefits hard assets with fixed supply.

📦

Trump Tariffs: 10% Global Levy

The Supreme Court ruled IEEPA does not authorize presidential tariffs, forcing a pivot to a 10% global tariff under Section 122 (effective Feb 24, 150-day window). Treasury Secretary Bessent signaled an increase to 15% is likely coming. The tariffs represent the largest US tax increase as a share of GDP since 1993 — an estimated $1,500 per household annually — weighing on consumer spending and corporate margins.

📉

Equities: S&P 500 Down 5.8% YTD

The S&P 500 fell 1.9% this week, closing Friday at 6,477. The same risk-off forces depressing equities are weighing on Bitcoin sentiment — but BTC's correlation to stocks in fear regimes has historically broken down on the recovery leg, with crypto tending to outperform once sentiment turns.

02

Bitcoin This Week

Bitcoin opened the week near $71,300 and closed Friday around $66,587 — a drop of approximately $4,700, or 6.6%. The immediate catalyst was a $14 billion options expiry event on Friday that flushed overleveraged longs and broke through the psychologically important $68,000 support level. This is a mechanical move, not a fundamental one.

Zooming out: BTC is sitting roughly 3.1% below its March 1 opening price with one week left in the month. If March closes red, it would mark six consecutive losing months — a streak that has historically been followed by sharp recoveries. Realized volatility has come down materially (from 80 to 50 annualized), and futures funding rates have dropped from 4.1% to 2.7%, suggesting leverage is being cleaned out of the market.

On the institutional side, US spot Bitcoin ETFs have posted three consecutive weeks of positive flows, with $340M net in the week of March 24 alone. BlackRock's IBIT and Fidelity's FBTC led with $215M and $95M respectively on March 24. This is the pattern that has preceded prior recoveries: smart money accumulating while retail sentiment is at its worst.

Strategy (formerly MicroStrategy) disclosed its largest Bitcoin purchase of 2026 this week — 22,337 BTC added, funded through preferred share issuance. Their total holdings now stand at approximately 762,000 BTC. The world's largest corporate Bitcoin holder is buying aggressively at these prices.

04

Outlook

The next 30 days will be shaped primarily by two variables: the trajectory of the Iran conflict and the Fed's flexibility if oil-driven inflation forces its hand. In the bearish scenario, energy costs stay elevated, the Fed delays cuts further, and risk assets face continued pressure. In the bullish scenario, a diplomatic resolution reduces the oil shock, inflation expectations stabilize, and the pent-up demand from extreme-fear buyers drives a sharp recovery — with Bitcoin likely leading.

The data we watch most closely is leaning toward the bullish setup: institutional ETF inflows are holding, leverage is being cleaned out, and corporate accumulation (via Strategy) continues uninterrupted. The accumulation signal is firing. Patient capital is being rewarded with better entry prices each week. We will have more to share in next week's letter.
