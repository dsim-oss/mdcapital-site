Featured: Strategy’s STRC Engine

The most consequential institutional development of the week was almost mechanical: between April 6 and April 12, Strategy added 13,927 BTC at an average price of approximately $71,902, a roughly $1B purchase funded almost entirely through their STRC preferred-stock at-the-market facility. The ATM pulled in $1.76B of new liquidity this week and STRC closed the week within pennies of par. Total Strategy holdings now sit at 780,897 BTC, with a cumulative cost basis near $58B.

This is the cleanest illustration we’ve had of the STRC flywheel in action. Strategy issues preferred stock to yield-seeking institutional buyers, takes the proceeds, buys spot BTC, and the resulting balance-sheet expansion is the input to MSTR common’s intrinsic NAV. When MSTR common trades at a discount to that NAV — as it has all week, at roughly −19% — the equity is, in effect, BTC at a markdown. The STRC dividend was confirmed at 11.5% annualized for April, payable April 30, which remains the structural reason we hold STRC at all: dollar-yielding instrument that funds the BTC accumulation we believe in.

A Note on the Tax Character of STRC Distributions

STRC monthly distributions are classified by Strategy as return of capital rather than ordinary dividend income. Mechanically, this means each distribution reduces the cost basis of the position rather than being recognized as current-year taxable income. For a taxable investor, the after-tax yield profile is materially more favorable than an equivalent-coupon ordinary dividend: the income is effectively deferred, converted into a basis reduction that only crystallizes when the position is sold. Our YTD attribution table above reflects this — the $70.80 in STRC distributions received YTD is accounted for as a basis adjustment against the $6,659 gross paid (giving the $98.33/sh adjusted basis on the IBKR statement), not as a separate income line. We will revisit the tax-character point in the year-end letter when the 1099 arrives.
