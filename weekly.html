Mike, happy Friday. It has been a week defined by macro headwinds rather than crypto-specific weakness — and that distinction matters for how we think about what comes next. Bitcoin is down roughly 6.6% on the week, but the broader context paints this as a macro-driven repricing, not a structural deterioration. 01

Macro Environment

🛢️

Iran Conflict & Oil Shock — The Dominant Macro Story

The US-Israel strikes on Iran that began February 28 have reshuffled the entire macro deck. Brent crude has surged more than 40% since the conflict started, trading near $104/barrel this week — and the partial disruption to flows through the Strait of Hormuz has the IEA calling this the largest supply disruption in the history of the global oil market. Markets whipsawed Monday (+1.15% on the S&P) when Trump announced a temporary halt to strikes on Iranian energy infrastructure, then gave most of it back by Thursday. Elevated energy costs feed directly into inflation and complicate the Fed's path.

🏦

Federal Reserve: Rates on Hold, Inflation Forecast Raised

The FOMC held rates unchanged at its March 18 meeting — the second consecutive hold of 2026. Their updated projections raised the 2026 inflation forecast to 2.7% (from 2.5% in December) and still call for just one 25bp rate cut in 2026, with another in 2027. Real GDP is projected at 2.4% for 2026. The longer-run neutral rate was nudged up to 3.1%. In plain terms: higher-for-longer is now the baseline, and the oil shock makes early cuts even less likely. This is historically a net-positive environment for hard assets like Bitcoin.

📦

Trump Tariffs: 10% Global Levy, USMCA Reprieve Extended

The Supreme Court ruled 6–3 that IEEPA does not authorize presidential tariffs, forcing the administration to pivot to a 10% global tariff under Section 122 (effective Feb 24, 150-day window). Treasury Secretary Bessent signaled an increase to 15% likely coming "sometime this week." On Monday, markets briefly rallied when Trump extended the USMCA deadline — but the structural drag on corporate margins and consumer purchasing power remains. The tariffs represent the largest US tax increase as a share of GDP since 1993, with an estimated $1,500 annual cost per household.

📉

Equities Slide — S&P 500 Down 5.8% YTD

The S&P 500 fell 1.9% this week, closing Friday at 6,477. The index is now down 5.8% year-to-date, battered by the combination of tariff fears (February) and energy/geopolitical shock (March). The risk-off environment that has weighed on equities is the same force depressing Bitcoin sentiment — but BTC's correlation to equities in fear regimes has historically broken down on the recovery leg, with crypto outperforming once sentiment turns.

Strategy Inc (MSTR / STRC) Update

Strategy had a notable week. The company disclosed its largest Bitcoin purchase of 2026 — adding 22,337 BTC, funded primarily through STRC preferred share issuance. Their total holdings now stand at approximately 762,000 BTC, a meaningful increase from the 738,750 figure in our prior analysis. At current prices, that represents a Bitcoin treasury worth roughly $50.7 billion against roughly 337.8 million diluted shares — implying an mNAV of ~$150 per share.

MSTR itself has been under pressure, with the stock trading in the $124–$134 range this week — oscillating between a mild discount and a meaningful one relative to underlying BTC value. At $124.85 (the reported intraday level on Friday), MSTR was trading at approximately −17% to mNAV, which approaches or crosses our −15% accumulation threshold. This warrants watching. We also note that a company director (Jarrod Patten) sold 700 shares on March 26 at $124.85 via an SEC-reported transaction — insider selling is always worth flagging, though at this scale it is likely routine.

On STRC: the preferred has recovered to $99.97 — essentially back at par — after briefly dipping below $100 earlier in the month. This confirms that our income harvest window from below-par purchases was correctly identified and has now closed. The 11.5% variable dividend continues to accrue.

05

Outlook & Positioning Thoughts

The next 30 days will likely be shaped by two variables above all others: the trajectory of the Iran conflict and the Fed's willingness to pivot if oil-driven inflation forces it into a corner. In the bearish scenario, elevated oil sustains inflation above target, the Fed delays cuts further, and risk assets including BTC face continued pressure. In the bullish scenario, a diplomatic resolution reduces the oil shock, inflation expectations stabilize, and the pent-up demand from extreme-fear buyers drives a sharp recovery — potentially with Bitcoin leading.

US spot Bitcoin ETF inflows have remained positive for three consecutive weeks ($340M net in the week of March 24 alone), suggesting that institutional buyers are using this fear environment to accumulate. This is the pattern that preceded prior recoveries. Strategy's own continued purchasing — funded by its preferred equity machine — adds to the structural bid.

For MD Capital, the near-term priority is capital allocation discipline: our signals tell us when to buy, not just whether conditions are favorable. The BTC accumulation signal is firing clearly. If MSTR's discount crosses −15% cleanly, we have a second signal active simultaneously — a rare and historically opportune combination. We will revisit deployment options in next week's check-in.

Thank you for your continued trust and patience. This is not the environment anyone hoped for in Q1 2026 — but it is, if we stay the course, exactly where long-term Bitcoin allocators are built.
