Market Context

This was a week of contradictions. Bitcoin reclaimed $70,000 with a quiet +1.3% bounce, yet the Fear & Greed Index collapsed from 23 to 11 — its lowest reading since the July 2025 washout. That kind of divergence between price stability and sentiment panic historically signals one thing: smart money is accumulating while retail runs scared.

The on-chain data backs this up. Whale wallets holding more than 1,000 BTC showed net inflows of 8,400 BTC over 48 hours, even as trading volume dipped below the 30-day average. This is textbook accumulation: low volume, stable price, extreme fear, large wallets quietly adding.

Meanwhile, Strategy (formerly MicroStrategy) continued its aggressive treasury build, purchasing 22,337 BTC last week for ~$1.57 billion at an average price of $70,194. That brings their total holdings to 761,068 BTC — funded primarily through $1.18B in STRC preferred share sales. They're on pace to hold 1 million BTC by year-end.

A quieter but significant milestone: Bitcoin's circulating supply crossed 20 million BTC around March 10. Only 1 million coins remain to be mined over the next ~114 years. The supply squeeze narrative is no longer forward-looking — it's here.

Strategy & Outlook
This Week's Thesis
Extreme fear with stable price is the definition of an accumulation window. We're not trying to time the bottom — we're buying into the fear because the structural case for Bitcoin hasn't changed: supply is shrinking, institutional demand is growing, and the monetary backdrop remains favorable. Our barbell strategy (spot BTC for upside + STRC for yield) lets us generate income while we wait.

The current drawdown of -15.9% from contributed capital is uncomfortable but expected given BTC's correction from the $105K highs. Our portfolio is designed for this: the STRC dividend stream provides real cash flow regardless of price action, and the spot BTC position gives us full upside when the cycle turns.

Looking ahead: We'll continue deploying cash into spot BTC via weekly purchases at Swan while the Fear & Greed Index stays in extreme fear territory. STRC accumulation pauses until IBKR is funded again. If the MSTR NAV discount crosses -15%, that becomes our next action item — but it would require wiring additional funds to IBKR.

The 12-month Monte Carlo simulation (10,000 runs, 60% annual BTC volatility, conservative 0% drift) puts the probability of full capital recovery at roughly 52-55%. Not a guarantee, but the distribution is positively skewed — the upside scenarios significantly outweigh the downside. Time and DCA are working in our favor.
